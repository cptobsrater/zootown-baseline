# ZooTown News — canonical script reference

This is the editorial gold standard for the daily ZooTown News video script,
provided by Cody on June 15, 2026. Every generated script must match this
voice, structure, and rhythm. The minute-script generator's prompt
embeds this verbatim as the model's reference.

## Structure (in order)

1. **Anchor intro** — warm greeting, sets the geographic frame
   (Missoula + Western Montana), pause cue + small smile cue,
   teases the segments, transitions to "tonight's top stories."

2. **Five story segments**, each:
   - One ~3-sentence body delivered as a clean anchor read.
   - Inline source attribution after each sentence, formatted as
     `[[source_slug](https://full.url/)]`.
   - One **(stage cue)** in italics after the body — tone/expression
     direction for the anchor delivery (small smile, beat, neutral,
     calm but serious, friendly outdoorsy, warm smile, etc.).
   - One short kicker sentence after the cue.

3. **Toss to weather / outro** — "Those are your top stories" line,
   sign-off, beat cue, "We'll be right back."

## Editorial rules

- Conversational but objective. American English. Contractions are fine.
- **Each story is its own self-contained segment.** Story 1 stays the
  Zootown Festival lead until that festival has happened or details
  change. Story 2 is its own thing. They don't blur together.
- A story's segment is held in rotation until the event happens OR
  the underlying details change. Same segment text reused across nights.
- Inline source attribution after each meaningful claim. Outlet's
  short slug in square brackets, full URL in parens.
- One stage cue per story (italicized in the markdown reference,
  in parentheses on its own line).
- Honest tone signaling — "calm but serious" for safety stories,
  "warm smile" for feel-good closers, "friendly outdoorsy" for
  rec/environment, "neutral, inviting" for civic.
- No invented facts. No fabricated names. Use `[BRACKETED PLACEHOLDERS]`
  when a real name or number isn't available yet.
- Banned phrases: "a testament to", "delving into", "shed light on",
  "navigate the complexities", "ever-evolving", "underscores",
  "moreover", "furthermore", "tapestry", "robust" (as filler),
  "in conclusion", "it is important to note".
- No em-dashes (HeyGen mis-paces them). Use commas or sentence
  breaks. (En-dashes in compound modifiers like "below-market-rate"
  are fine.)
- Toss-to-weather line CAN mention zootownhub.com — that's the
  destination, not the source. Story attributions never use
  zootownhub.com.

## Anchor name resolution

The script template uses `[Ryan Johnson]` as the anchor name placeholder.
Generated scripts must replace this with the actual on-camera anchor name
configured in `ZOOTOWN_MINUTE_CONFIG.anchor_name`. Currently: **Nicholas**.

## Segment persistence model

- Each story segment is identified by a stable `segment_key` (e.g.
  `zootown-festival-2026`, `housing-council-proposals-june-2026`).
- Segment stays in rotation until either:
  - the underlying event passes (calendar dependent), or
  - the source's reporting on it materially changes (a vote happens,
    a number is revised, a date moves).
- The daily script assembly picks the active segments by key, in priority
  order, and emits them with their persistent body text. No regeneration
  unless one of the trigger conditions fires.
